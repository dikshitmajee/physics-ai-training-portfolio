# Advanced Nuclear Spectroscopy Question

## Question

If an RDCO value is measured as 0.95 for a gamma transition gated on a quadrupole transition, can we confidently assign it as quadrupole? Explain the limitations.

## Discussion

RDCO values close to unity often indicate quadrupole transitions when gated on quadrupole transitions.

However, the interpretation is not always straightforward because:

• finite detector efficiency  
• statistical uncertainty  
• possible multipole mixing  
• incorrect gating transitions  

Therefore RDCO values must be interpreted together with additional information such as:

- polarization measurements (IPDCO)
- gamma-gamma coincidence relationships
- systematics of neighboring nuclei.

## Insight

This example demonstrates why experimental nuclear spectroscopy often requires multiple observables before assigning spin and parity.
